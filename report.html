What Happens When You Make a Report?

Prevent is not a criminal process. People who have been referred to Prevent are often approached for questioning or 'informal chats' by Counter-Terrorism Police (SO15) Officers, local police or social workers to discuss their political, cultural or religious beliefs. They check if there is an immediate security threat. They will also check if there is a genuine risk of radicalisation. If the person isn't at risk of radicalisation, they are not a case for Prevent.

If the reported individual is found to be at risk the Channel panel will meet and accept them itno the Channel program: a confidential program that offers tailored support and early intervention, including mental health services or social services.
